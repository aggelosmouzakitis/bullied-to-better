Bio

I'm a licensed psychotherapist, an executive coach and a tech Product Growth leader. I've spent 18 years in the industry as a founder, consultant, and corporate strategist working with 50+ startups and training 500+ people. I now work with senior professionals navigating the harder, less visible challenges that come with operating at a high level.

I'm not a coach who learned about tech by reading about it. I've lived it.

Who I Work With

I work with senior high-performers who are successful by most measures and struggling in ways they can't easily name.

Some are hitting walls they don't understand — repeating patterns, losing drive, making decisions that don't reflect who they think they are. Others are mid-transition: a new role, a company exit, a life that no longer fits. Some are dealing with the kind of anxiety and self-doubt that doesn't show up in their LinkedIn profile but follows them into every room.

What they have in common: they're not looking for cheerleading. They want someone who can actually see what's happening.

Background

18 years in tech taught me that most performance problems aren't strategy problems. They're identity problems, pattern problems, internal architecture problems. The org chart, the roadmap, the metrics — those are rarely where the real issue lives.

I trained as a psychotherapist because I kept hitting the ceiling of what strategic advice could do. Understanding the motives and causes underneath decisions changes everything. It's slower at first, and more durable afterward.

How I Work

I don't separate therapy from coaching artificially. What I offer depends on what the situation actually needs — sometimes that's therapeutic work, sometimes career or leadership coaching, sometimes a direct strategic conversation. I hold all of it.

My approach is precise, not gentle. I move toward what's difficult, not around it. I don't rush to reframes or action plans before we've understood what's actually driving the pattern. That's how lasting change works.
